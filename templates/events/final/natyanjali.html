THEMATIC
“Art in motion”
Team composition:8-15 members+ 3 (backstage)
Teams per college: Unlimited
Registration: Online/On the spot
Time limit: 6-10 minutes

General Rules

-A minimum of 6 dancers need to be present on the stage at all times.
-A college/institute can participate in both thematic and non-thematic but the same crew cannot perform in both.
-Use of props is allowed and may fetch extra points. However, it is not necessary.
-The Organizing Committee must be notified of the same at least 1 day prior to the performance by sending a mail to events@bits-waves.org. The subject of the mail should be “Request to use props for Natyanjali”.
-A tiebreaker round might be introduced after the eliminations, only if found necessary by the judges and the event managers. The rules for the tie breaker (if necessary) will be disclosed at the end of the elimination round.
-Hence, at least one member from each group must stay back till the result of the elimination round is announced.

Event Rules

Elimination Round:
- Participants will be required to perform a prepared dance sequence.
- The duration of this performance will be 6-10 minutes.
- 5% of the total score will be deducted for each minute above the time limit.
- Participants can incorporate any dance form into their performance as long as it is conveying a story or a message.
- The elimination round is not open to watch, and will be held in a closed room.
Only the judging panel will be viewing the team’s performance.

Final Round:
- The teams who qualify for the final will have to perform the same dance sequence as the elimination round. The final round will be held in the auditorium in front of a live audience.
- Rules and guidelines will be same as those for the elimination round.
- The auditorium screen shall not be used for any purpose (photos, videos, etc.)
- Teams are not allowed to use any external lighting for their performance.

Judging Criteria:
- Costumes
- Props used
- Expressions
- Choreography
- Coordination/synchronization
- Originality of idea
- Dramatic interpretation
- Choreography will be given the highest amount of priority while awarding points.
